🛍️ Shopping

Bucharest — malls

Free entry Big, air-conditioned, great on a hot afternoon.
  • AFI Cotroceni — the handiest big mall; Primark, H&M, Zara, kids’ stores, cinema, huge food court. (Also Gymboland & Museum of Senses next door.)
  • Băneasa Shopping City — more upscale brands, in the north.
  • Mega Mall / ParkLake / Sun Plaza — other large malls if you’re nearby.

Souvenirs & local goods

  • Old Town (Lipscani) — boutiques, cafés, souvenirs, ceramics.
  • Bran Castle market — honey, wool, painted eggs, ceramics and (of course) Dracula kitsch.
  • Take home: Romanian honey, ceramics, wine (Cotnari, Murfatlar), and sweets.

✅ Good to know

  • Bring water & snacks on every outing — caves, cable cars, coaster and castle queues.
  • Cash (RON) for parking, the coaster and some caves; ATMs everywhere; cards accepted in cities.
  • Peleș Castle closed Mon & Tue — go Sunday Aug 9.
  • Go early — Bran, Peleș and the cable cars fill up midday in August.
  • Mountain weather shifts fast; pack layers, a light rain jacket, sun hats & sunscreen.
  • Winding roads in the Carpathians — bring motion-sickness remedies for the kids.
  • Road vignette (rovinietă) — confirm it’s included with Klass Wagen; check booster seats for Ari & Omer.
  • Offline map — download the Prahova/Brașov area in Google Maps; signal is patchy in the mountains.
  • Pharmacies (farmacie): Catena, Sensiblu, some 24h. Emergency: 112.
  • Power: Type C/F, 230 V — bring an EU adapter from Israel.
  • SIM/data: cheap prepaid (Vodafone/Orange/Digi) at the airport or malls.
  • Tipping ~10% in restaurants. Tap water is fine in cities; many still prefer bottled.

🧒 For Ari & Omer

  • Sure hits: Dino Parc Râșnov, the alpine coaster, the cable cars, Therme (slides & warm pools), Museum of Senses and Gymboland.
  • Comfortable shoes for cobblestones and short mountain trails.
  • A small day-pack: water, snacks, hats, a layer, wet wipes, and coins for toilets.
  • Therme is a great “reset” day before the flight home.
